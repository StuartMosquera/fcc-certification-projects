Carl Sagan

Astronomer, astrophysicist and scientific communicator

Here’s a time line of Carl Sagan’s life:

  • 1934 - Born in Brooklyn, New York
  • 1949 - At age 14, visits the Hayden Planetarium, sparking his interest in astronomy
  • 1951 - Graduates from high school
  • 1955 - Enters the University of Chicago, where he earns his bachelor’s degree in physics
  • 1960 - Obtains his master’s degree in astronomy and astrophysics from the University of Chicago
  • 1960 - Begins working in NASA’s Planetary Exploration Program
  • 1966 - Becomes a professor of astronomy and space sciences at Cornell University
  • 1970 - Publishes “The Dragons of Eden,” which wins Pulitzer Prize
  • 1980 - Premiere of the television series “Cosmos: A Personal Journey,” which becomes a great success and popularizes him
  • 1995 - Publishes his book “The Demon-Haunted World,” where he advocates for critical thinking and scientific skepticism
  • 1996 - Dies at the age of 62.

“Every one of us is, in the cosmic perspective, precious. If a human disagrees with you, let him live. In a hundred billion galaxies, you will not find another.”

-- Carl Sagan
